How do I play Jailbreak?

So you've stumbled upon the world of Jailbreak, but you don't know where to get started with the gamemode... Don't worry, friend, I'll try and help you get your foot in the door! Although it may seem intimidating at first, once you get the hang of it, Jailbreak can be a lot of fun! Don't suffice this tutorial for a full introduction to our server, though, as you should always read the rules before playing!

To start off, Jailbreak consists of two primary teams; these are the prisoners, who spawn in cells and follow the warden's orders, and the guards who manage the prison! The guards team, and especially the warden, are in charge of orchestrating the days, keeping track of prisoners, and most importantly, creating a fun experience for the prisoners team. The prisoners, on the other hand, have the option of participating in the warden's days and attempting to be the last prisoner alive, or if they dislike the day being played, rebelling, and attempting to eliminate the guards team!

Whilst on the guards team, you must keep track of the prisoners during the day, and ensure that no prisoners try and rebel. You must follow the orders of the warden at all times, or alternatively, you may type "/warden", or hit F4, at the start of the round to claim warden yourself! As the warden, your role is to create games and activities for the prsioners to try and bring the prisoner count to one, whom then gets a Last Request, or LR. It's recommended you get experience before going warden, so watch what other wardens do, and spend some time on the prisoners team first!

Speaking of prisoners... As a prisoner, you have the choice of following the warden's orders and trying to survive through the day, or rebelling, and attempting to eliminate the guards team. The warden will try different tricks to pick off prisoners, often even whilst you're in your cell, so be cunning and listen closely to what the warden says! If you manage to become the last prisoner left alive, you will get an LR, which allows you to suggest something to be played the following day, although it may be denied; alternatively, you can hit F4 to pit yourself one-on-one with a guard, and try to pick off the remaining guards to win the round!

Hopefully this small introduction has helped you get more comfortable with the gamemode, and I wish you, dear reader, lots of fun whilst playing!

General Rules

  • Do not mic spam or flood the chat box.
  • Do not evade punishments by leaving and rejoining the server; this'll only make things worse for you.
  • When a staff member asks you to stop doing something you stop, no questions asked. Staff words are final, so refrain from fighting back, and feel free to make a demotion report on the forums if you feel a staff member has done something wrong.
  • Do not release the personal information of players without their explicit consent.
  • No NSFW sprays; this includes shock materal (gore, torture, etc.), non human/adult nudity, and sexual acts.
  • Do not use hacks, scripts, bugs, or any other exploits to gain an advantage over other players; This includes misuse of the !stuck command.
  • Any serious threats to the server, such as DDOS threats or attempts to crash the server, will result in an immediate permanent ban. Don't do it.

Jailbreak Rules

  1. Use your common sense. If you feel like you're doing something wrong, stop.
  2. Freekilling/RDM/Teamkilling will not be tolerated.
  3. Do not give intentional "freedays" under any circumstances.
    • This excludes "first day freeday" after a map change.
    • Freedays may not be given as a Last Request.
  4. Do not intentionally stall or delay the round (this includes cell days) or hide and wait for prolonged periods (camping).
  5. The Warden must communicate appropriately, whether it be by talking over voice chat or placing waypoints; if you can't communicate clearly, don't become the Warden.
    • Waypoints may be placed by holding C (default). Note: Waypoints do not count as specific orders, and must be accompanied with a command.
    • It is adviced you refrain from becoming the Warden if your mic is of poor quality or you are hard to understand, all it does is make it a pain for the people trying to listen to you and carry out your orders.
    • To avoid parts of your command being cut out, beging holding down your voice chat key before you give your command, and release it after you've finished speaking.
  6. Guards must obey and protect the Warden at all times; do what he says when he says it.
  7. Prisoners and guards may refuse an order that will lead to certain death.
    • If you are ordered to jump off a building, or do anything else that will endanger your life, you may refuse the order.
    • Orders that lead to the possibility of death may not be refused (i.e. certain games or days that kill off prisoners).
  8. Guards are not allowed to gunplant; do not bring and distribute weapons to prisoners under any circumstances. Failure to follow this rule will result in a lengthy ban.
    • It is recommended you enable "double press to drop" in the settings (accessible by pressing F1) to avoid accidentally gunplanting.
  9. Do not ghost/metagame. If you are dead, do not give the prisoners that are still alive any information that may have an effect on the day.
    • This includes answers to trivia questions; it's a test of their wit, not yours.
    • Do not give rebellers hints that may heighten their chance at success, such as when hallways are clear, guard locations, or telling them escape routes. The reverse applies; don't tell guards the locations of rebelling prisoners.
    • "Fake ghosting" may still be considered ghosting if it has an effect on the day.
  10. Guards cannot interfere with a prisoner's Last Request; if they are having a battle, do not shoot the prisoner.
    • However, if the prisoner is targeting guards other than their LR opponent, this counts as rebelling and the prisoner can be KOS.
    • If a prisoner was rebelling before they initiated Last Request they may still be KOS.
  11. Kill prisoners within reason. If they have a knife, don't just gun them down, you must ask them to drop it first.
    • If a prisoner has a gun, holstered or not, they may be KOS.
  12. You are not allowed to camp the armory, once you have chosen your weaponry you should leave it.
  13. Do not close cells once they have been opened, unless prisoners are to enter them later in the day.
  14. If a Warden has not given an order before 60 seconds after the start of a round, the day becomes a freeday.
    • If this occurs, do not keep prisoners in their cells, open the cells if they don't open automatically.
    • Cell orders (such as touching and facing cell doors) count as orders.
  15. You cannot prevent prisoners from doing Last Request, and neither player may use the medic bay or health kits during Last Request.
    • If a day is still in progress (i.e. "the first prisoner to reach the end gets LR"), but everyone else dies, the Warden may refuse to give Last Request until the day is complete.
  16. All guards must communicate clearly with working microphones (this is based on admin discretion), and all guards must be willing to go Warden. Refer to rule no. 5.
    • You must have, and use, a working microphone to be on the guards team.
    • If you are not willing to take the Warden position, don't be on the guards team.
    • Guards may only give simple orders, such as "drop your weapon" or "get back in line", leave the complex orders to the Warden.
  17. If there are conflicting orders, the most recent order takes priority.
    • For example, if the Warden says leaving the circle is KOS, then instructs the prisoners to move to the square, you may not KOS prisoners for leaving the circle.
    • This rule is excepted during Simon Says, so listen closely. For example, Simon must say that leaving the circle is no longer KOS.
  18. If you are the last guard alive you may KOS anyone who is holding a weapon; you may also try to take the position of Warden and give new orders - if prisoners do not comply they are rebelling (KOS).
    • The last guard may not "kill all" without a valid, rule-abiding reason, such as a warday, or the prisoners are rebelling.
  19. We aren't here to simply win rounds, but to allow both teams to have fun. Don't make killing everyone your main objective, but instead, try to have fun and give everyone a chance to contribute and survive. This applies to both the prisoner team and the guard team.
    • If you are on the prisoner team, don't rebel every round. Rebelling is a healthy component of the game, but in excess, is simply not fun.
    • Try to stray away from games and activities that remove a large group of prisoners purely on chance, it's not fun to miss out on a day simply because the dice wasn't in your favour.
    • Furthermore, days which may be considered cruel and unfair for prisoners, such as Faze Clan recruitment day, are prohibited.
  20. No prop surfing or killing.
  21. Do not repeat days/games (i.e. don't play three wardays in a row).
  22. Cell days are prohibited.
    • Prisoners must be out of their cells by one and a half minutes after the start of the round.
    • Cells may be used for games later in the day, but not in excess.
  23. Games or days that require the use of the developer console are prohibited.
  24. As a guard, do not open the cell doors without your Warden's permission.
  25. First day special days which involve prisoners spawning with a weapon are to be treated as wardays; any other first day special day will continute to be considered a freeday.
  26. Cheap tricks which are used to confuse and kill off prisoners are prohibited.
    • For example, unclear instructions, conflicting instructions/waypoints, or talking in a way that is too fast or unclear.
  27. If any invincible props, such as barricades, are being used to prop-block an area, item pickup must be enabled.

Jailbreak Glossary

Commands

  1. AFK Freeze
    • While AFK frozen you are not allowed to move in any direction, jump, crouch, look around, have your fists rasied, use your mic, or use your flashlight (it must be turned off). Simply, just don't touch your keyboard and mouse.
    • Text chatting is allowed, and can never be prevented by the warden, i.e. "text chatting is KOS".
    • No, just because your mic key is bound to your mouse doesn't mean you can use it. The guards don't know that.
  2. Freeze
    • Anything is allowed except pressent any of your movement keys, crouching, or jumping (WASD, CTRL and space by default).
  3. Crouch Walk
    • Move whilst holding your crouch key (bound to left control by default).
  4. Alt Walk
    • Move whilst holding your walk key (bound to alt by default).
  5. Unstack Shoulder-to-Shoulder
    • Don't stack (stand inside other players)and stand shoulder-to-shoulder with the other prisoners in a line.
    • The Warden has to specify a place to do this, i.e. "unstack shoulder-to-shoulder in front of cell stairs".
  6. No Detours or Delays
    • If the Warden tells a prisoner to move somewhere, they must not detour or delay or they will be KOS; this always applies, unless the Warden specifies a time to get there, then you can take your time.
    • The Warden does not have to say no detours or delays for it to apply.

Special Days

  1. Free Day
    • If the Warden dies, or the Warden hasn't given an order before one minute passes, the day becomes a freeday.
    • Prisoners may do whatever they like, as long as they do not pick up weapons or enter KOS zones (vents, armory, etc.).
  2. Hide and Seek
    • Once the warden has specified the current round is going to be a hide and seek day, all guards must stay in the armory, excluding one guard that should open cell doors. Once cell doors are open, the prisoners must run out of their cells and find somewhere to hide on the map.
    • When the timer hits the time specified by the Warden earlier, the guards may leave the armory and begin looking for prisoners. Guards may not kill prisoners before the specified time unless the prisoner is in the armory.
  3. Reverse Hide and Seek
    • The opposite of a hide and seek day; all guards go hide, except for one who stays back to open cell doors and run (unless the map automatically opens cell doors).
    • All guards must use either their melee or secondary weapons on a reverse hide and seek day, otherwise it's just a warday with camping.
  4. War Day
    • The Warden must specify a time, and once the time has been reached, all prisoners are KOS.
    • The guards must keep wardays fair for prisoners, so the guards must allow prisoners to enter the armory and get guns.
    • Avoid camping on this day, save that for reverse hide and seek.
  5. Lava Day
    • The floor is lava! After a specified time, all prisoners must be off of the floor, and/or on an object in their cell, otherwise they are KOS.
    • Lava day ends once prisoners are instructed to leave, except during Simon Says.
  6. Extermination/Execution Day
    • After a specified time, all prisoners that can be seen in their cells are KOS, so prisoners must hide behind objects or dividers in their cells.
    • A guard must never enter a cell on an extermination day.
    • This day can only be played on maps that have hiding spots in every cell.
  7. Shrek Swamp
    • The Warden will claim a certain area to be the 'swamp', and all guards must stay in the swamp, defending it from prisoners.
    • The guards must not leave the swamp, nor kill any prisoners whilst outside of the designated swamp; doing so is considered RDM and will be dealt with accordingly.
    • Prisoners must be let out of their cells and permitted to get guns.

Common Games

  1. First Reaction Last Reaction
    • If the Warden states "first reaction (command)", the first person to do it will be KOS; if the Warden states "last reaction (command)", the last person to do it will be KOS.
    • Once a prisoner has died all remaining prisoners should reset facing the Warden unless stated otherwise.
    • The Warden must state clearly that the prisoners are playing First Reaction Last Reaction.
  2. Simon Says
    • The Warden must make it clear to all prisoners that they are now playing Simon Says. Once in effect, simon says is not over until Simon says it is.
    • If the Warden gives an order by saying "Simon says (order, e.g. crouch)", all prisoners must do this order or they are KOS.
    • If the Warden gives an order without saying "Simon says", doing the order is KOS.
  3. Shoot or Don't Shoot
    • All prisoners must face a wall lined up shoulder-to-shoulder; the Warden then points their gun at a prisoner or the ceiling, and asks either that prisoner or another prisoner "shoot or don't shoot". The prisoner that has been asked gets to choose whether the gun is fired or not, often choosing another prisoners fate.
    • However, if the Warden is aiming at the ceiling, the prisoner who has been asked "shoot or don't shoot" dies instead.
    • Please note, there are other ways of playing this, but this is the most fair way to play; other ways may result in the Warden being punished for favouritism.
  4. Snitch
    • When two prisoners remain, the Warden may initiate snitch to decide LR; both prisoners should stand back-to-back, and once the Warden says "snitch", prisoners decide whether to crouch or remain standing.
    • If one prisoner crouches, the standing prisoner will be KOS; If both prisoners crouch, both prisoners are KOS. Neither prisoner will die if both prisoners are standing.
  5. Spot the (Blank)
    • If three prisoners remain, the Warden may decide to play spot the (blank, often called spot the faggot). The remaining three prisoners stand in a triangle formation, facing away from eachother, turning around to face another prisoner once the Warden says "spot the (blank)".
    • The prisoner that has two prisoners looking at him will be KOS.

Important Words and Abbreviations

  1. Rebel
    • A prisoner who isn't following the Warden's orders.
    • Rebellers are automatically KOS, unless they are pardoned by the Warden.
  2. KOS
    • Kill on Sight; if a guard spots a prisoner considered KOS they may shoot them.
  3. LR
    • Last Request; provided they didn't rebel and it is not a warday, the last prisoner alive gets Last Request.
    • The last prisoner may request a game/day to be palyed tomorrow if a Warden is alive, or alternatively, may challenge the guards to a trivia or a duel by pressing F4, killing all the guards one by one and winning the round for the prisoners.
    • A prisoner that kills a guard during their Last Request duel is not KOS.
  4. Pardon
    • A prisoner that has rebelled may request to be pardoned by the Warden, allowing them to join back in on the game/day being played.
    • The Warden may refuse to pardon a prisoner, but must allow all prisoners equal rights; the Warden may not pardon one prisoner, but not another, who have rebelled in the same way.
  5. KOS Area
    • A KOS area is an area that is off-limits to prisoners; entering one of these areas is considered rebelling and the prisoner is KOS, hence the name.
    • This includes hidden passages, hidden vents, vents, the armory, guard towers, and catwalks intended for guard use.

Chat commands for users

  1. !menu
    • Opens the ULX menu, which shows commands available to you.
  2. !rtv
    • Starts a map prevote, which will begin a mapvote at the end of the round once reached.
    • If you dislike the map being played, feel free to !rtv, and if others don't like it either you may be able to vote on a preferred map!
  3. @
    • Type @ before your message to contact an admin (e.g. @ (player) is spamming).
  4. !report/rdm/wtf rdm
    • Type !report (or rdm/wtf rdm) in chat to open the report menu; after filling in the report template, it will be sent to a staff member, who will read your report and act appropriately.
    • Please use the report menu for it's intended use; fill in the provided template and don't make false reports. Misuse of the report function will result in harsh punishments.
  5. !p (player)
    • You can message another player privately by typing !p, followed by their username and a message (e.g. !p "player" How are you today?).
    • Chat spam rules still apply to private messages.
  6. !stuck/!unstuck
    • If you are stuck in a wall, object, or another player, you may use the !stuck command to free yourself.
    • Misuse of this command will result in harsh punishment.
  7. !donate
    • Opens the donation window, which allows you to donate, helping us keep the server alive whilst receiving some in-game perks!
    • The donation page can also be accessed from the Poseidon forums, or from the top of this page!

Function keys

  1. F1
    • Gains access to a brief introduction to the gamemode, game options and round logs when you are dead.
  2. F2
    • Press this to switch between guard, prisoner and spectator.
  3. F3
    • Opens the Pointshop, where you can spend points earnt from playing the game for cosmetic items and powerups!
    • Items marked with a gold star are reserved for donators.
  4. F4
    • As a prisoner, this opens the Last Request menu if you are the last prisoner alive.
    • As a guard, allows you to claim Warden at the start of the round, or if you're the last guard left; you may also take warden by typing /warden!
